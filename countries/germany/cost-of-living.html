Cost of Living in Germany

Estimated Monthly Living Costs

Single Person: Approx. €1,600–€2,500

Family (2 adults + 2 children): Approx. €3,200–€4,900

Actual costs depend heavily on the city (Munich/Frankfurt are high), lifestyle, and housing type.

Housing (Warmmiete vs. Kaltmiete)

German rental prices are often quoted as Kaltmiete (cold rent - base rent) and Warmmiete (warm rent - includes ancillary costs like heating, water, and garbage). Be sure to check what is included.

  • 1-bedroom apartment (Kaltmiete): €700–€1,200/month (Lower in East/smaller cities, higher in Munich/Frankfurt)
  • 3-bedroom apartment (Kaltmiete): €1,400–€2,500/month in urban areas
  • Utilities (Electricity/Internet/Phone): Typically paid separately from Warmmiete.

Utilities & Internet

  • Electricity, water, gas (included in Warmmiete prepayments, but billed based on usage): €150–€250/month
  • Separate Electricity (if not included): €50–€100/month
  • Internet (High-speed fiber): €35–€50/month
  • **TV/Radio Licence Fee (Rundfunkbeitrag):** Mandatory €18.50/month per household.

Food & Groceries

Germany is known for competitive grocery prices, especially at discount supermarkets like **Aldi** and **Lidl**. Eating out, however, can be costly.

  • Groceries (Single person budget): €250–€350 per month
  • Groceries (Family of 4 budget): €550–€900 per month
  • Restaurant meal (Inexpensive): €12–€20 per person

Healthcare & Social Security

In Germany, statutory (public) health insurance is mandatory for employees below a high income threshold, with costs linked to your gross salary (shared by employer and employee).

  • Statutory Health Insurance (Employee share): Approx. 7.3% of gross income (up to the income threshold)
  • Total Social Security (Health, Pension, Unemployment, Care): Approx. 20-22% of gross income (employee share)

Read more: Understanding Social Security and Health Insurance in Germany

Transportation

The **Deutschlandticket** (€49–€63/month) provides excellent, nationwide, unlimited access to regional and local public transport.

  • Deutschlandticket (Monthly Pass): Approx. €63/month (Nationwide)
  • Car ownership: Expensive due to high fuel (€1.70–€2.00/litre), insurance, and tax costs.
  • Biking is extremely common and an affordable way to commute.

Miscellaneous & Leisure

  • Gym membership: €25–€50/month
  • Leisure, dining out, subscriptions: €150–€300/month
  • Childcare (Kita fees): Varies greatly; free or low-cost in some states, up to €300/month in others.
