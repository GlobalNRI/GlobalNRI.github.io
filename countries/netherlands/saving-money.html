How to Save Money in the Netherlands

Smart, practical ways to reduce everyday costs — food, dining out, shopping, transport, utilities and subscriptions.

Food & Dining

  • Use meal-deal apps: Too Good To Go (leftover meals), SocialDeal and Groupon for discounts.
  • Look for early-evening restaurant deals and weekday lunch menus — check local Facebook groups and restaurant newsletters.
  • Eat at canteens or university restaurants (cheaper) and try local markets for meals-on-the-go.
  • Bring refillable water bottle and avoid buying bottled water in shops.

Groceries & Shopping

  • Compare supermarkets: Albert Heijn, Jumbo, Lidl and Aldi — Lidl/Aldi are usually cheapest for basics.
  • Use supermarket loyalty apps: AH Bonus, Jumbo Bonuskaart, and check weekly flyers for discounts.
  • Shop own-brand items and seasonal produce; buy in bulk for non-perishables.
  • Use discount apps/websites and cashback extensions; consider subscription services only if you use them often.
  • Second-hand: Marktplaats, Facebook Marketplace, and local thrift stores (kringloopwinkel) for furniture and clothes.

Transport

  • Bike where possible — free, healthy and the most common way to save on short trips.
  • OV-chipkaart: use off-peak discounts and check NS discount passes if commuting long-distance.
  • Compare monthly vs single-ride passes; use regional providers (Arriva, Connexxion) for local promos.
  • Car sharing and carpooling for occasional trips (SnappCar, BlaBlaCar).

Utilities & Subscriptions

  • Compare energy suppliers annually — small changes can save €100s/year. Use comparison sites such as Pricewise.
  • Reduce heating and optimise insulation; set thermostat timers and use energy-efficient appliances.
  • Bundle or cancel unused subscriptions (streaming, gym) — review bank statements monthly.

Banking & Fees

  • Choose a bank with low monthly fees and free debit card withdrawals across major ATMs.
  • Use contactless or mobile payments to avoid foreign transaction fees (if travelling).

Insurance & Healthcare

  • Shop health-insurance premiums at comparison sites; consider voluntary deductible to lower monthly costs.
  • Compare contents and liability insurance; increase excess to reduce premiums if comfortable.

Entertainment & Lifestyle

  • Use student or youth discounts where available; check musea discount days and city cards for bundled savings.
  • Use library memberships for free media and community events.

Apps & Sites to Save

  • Too Good To Go — discounted leftover meals.
  • Marktplaats & Facebook Marketplace — second-hand goods.
  • Independer / Pricewise — compare utilities and insurance.
  • AH Bonus / Jumbo / Lidl weekly flyers — supermarket deals.

Seasonal & Tactical Tips

  • Buy winter clothes on end-of-season sales; buy summer items in autumn/winter sales.
  • Shop tax-free / duty-free when eligible (tourists with VAT refund rules).
