Send Money to India from the Netherlands

How to get the best exchange rate, reduce fees, and which services and trackers to use.

Quick Checklist

  • Compare exchange rate + transfer fees (not just advertised rate)
  • Use regulated providers (Wise, Revolut, Wise, TransferGo, Remitly)
  • Set rate alerts or use limit orders for large transfers
  • Avoid weekend transfers (rates may be worse; FX markets closed)

How to Get a Better Exchange Rate

  1. Compare total cost: Look at the delivered INR amount after fees, not just the FX rate.
  2. Use mid-market rate providers: Wise and Revolut typically use close-to-mid-market rates and show fees clearly.
  3. Use limit orders / rate alerts: Many providers let you set a target rate and execute when reached.
  4. Batch transfers: Combine smaller transfers into one to reduce fixed fees.
  5. Choose the right payout method: Bank-to-bank often has lower fees than cash pickup.
  6. Time your transfer: Transfer during European market hours for tighter spreads; avoid bank holidays.

Where to Track Exchange Rates

Use one or more of these reliable sources and set alerts:

  • XE.com — live mid-market rates and mobile alerts.
  • OANDA — historical rates and API access.
  • Wise (TransferWise) rates — see the real rate you will get from Wise.
  • Revolut — in-app rates and notifications for account holders.
  • Google Finance — quick reference and news.
  • Investing.com — charting and alerts.

Recommended Providers

Pick a provider based on cost, speed and payout options:

  • Wise — transparent fees, mid-market rates, bank transfers.
  • Revolut — good for personal users with app convenience and competitive rates.
  • Remitly — fast transfers, sometimes higher fees for speed.
  • TransferGo — competitive for bank transfers to India.
  • Traditional banks — convenient but usually more expensive; ask about margins and fees.

Practical Tips & Compliance

  • Check RBI rules for inward remittances in India — some amounts require documentation.
  • Double-check recipient details (IFSC, account number) to avoid delays or returns.
  • Keep transfer receipts and exchange confirmations for tax or bank queries.
