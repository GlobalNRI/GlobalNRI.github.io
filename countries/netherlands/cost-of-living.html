Cost of Living in the Netherlands

Estimated Monthly Living Costs

Single Person: Approx. €1,940–€2,620

Family (2 adults + 2 children): Approx. €3,780–€5,550

Actual costs depend on lifestyle, city, housing choices, and energy prices.

Housing

  • 1‑bedroom apartment: €1,200–€1,500/month, Amsterdam and Utrecht at the high end
  • 3‑bedroom apartment: €2,200–€3,200/month in major cities; cheaper in smaller towns

Utilities & Internet

  • Electricity, water, gas: €150–€240/month depending on usage and weather
  • Internet & phone bundle: €30–€60/month

Food & Groceries

Grocery prices in the Netherlands have increased significantly over the last few years. Indian groceries are generally more expensive compared to regular Dutch supermarkets.

You can read our guide on how to save money while shopping for groceries in the Netherlands.

  • Groceries: €260–€340 per person/month
  • Restaurant meal: €15–€30 per person (higher in Indian restaurants)

Indian restaurants are usually more expensive. A single meal typically costs €20–€30 per person.

Healthcare Insurance

  • Health insurance: €120–€150 per adult/month (basic package)

Read more: More information on Health Insurance in the Netherlands

Transportation

  • Public transport subscription: €80–€110/month (varies by region)
  • Biking is common and inexpensive
  • Fuel for cars: €1.90–€2.30/litre

Miscellaneous & Leisure

  • Leisure, dining out, subscriptions: €100–€220/month
  • Entertainment (cinema, events): €10–€25 per visit
