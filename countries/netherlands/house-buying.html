House Buying in the Netherlands (2026)

Buying a house in the Netherlands involves three main steps: finding a suitable house, arranging finance, and optionally making repairs or changes. Below is a structured guide to help NRIs and expats navigate this process.

1. Finding a Suitable House

  • Consult a loan adviser (hypotheek) to determine your maximum eligible loan amount.
  • Search houses on funda.nl to shortlist properties.
  • Contact the selling agent (makelaar) via the website for a viewing appointment.
  • If the house does not meet your needs, you can simply decline after the viewing.
  • If interested, consider a second or third viewing to confirm your decision.
  • Optionally hire a technical inspector to check for structural or maintenance issues.
  • Negotiate the price — you may hire a buying agent for expert advice or negotiate yourself.
  • Give oral consent to buy. The agent will prepare a contract for signature, giving you 3 days to reconsider without penalty.
  • The contract also allows time to arrange financing; if the bank denies the loan, the contract is cancelled without penalty.

2. Finance Arrangement

  • Contact a bank or hypotheek adviser. Provide financial details, employment info, and documents including the werkgeververklaring.
  • The adviser will determine the interest rate based on residence status, house value, contract length, citizenship/PR status, and down payment.
  • Loan duration is usually 30 years; interest can be fixed, floating, or a combination.
  • Bank approval may take 3–6 weeks.
  • During loan processing, arrange transfers for gas, water, electricity, internet, and house insurance.
  • Once approved, funds are transferred to the seller at the notary, who ensures legal compliance.
  • Before signing, revisit the house to confirm condition. Any deviations can be addressed before finalizing.
  • If not proficient in Dutch, a translator is mandatory for notary proceedings.
  • After signing and funds transfer, you receive the keys — make sure house insurance is arranged beforehand.

3. House Repairs & Changes

  • Check thoroughly during second/third viewings for additional investments needed: leaks, appliances, paint, walls, etc.
  • Expect the house to differ from the viewing — curtains, flooring, furniture, and wall conditions may not match previous visits.
  • Repairs may involve multiple parties: movers, flooring experts, heating specialists, painters, etc.
  • Insurance covers damages caused by repairs or accidents, but minor maintenance costs are yours.

4. Caution Before Buying

  • Reselling may take time — some houses sell quickly, others take years. Consider staying for 4+ years before buying.
  • Plan for ongoing maintenance costs and potential additional investments.
  • Always confirm house condition, especially for older properties.

Summary

Buying a house in the Netherlands requires preparation, patience, and attention to detail. Use professional advice where needed, verify all legal and financial steps, and plan for maintenance and insurance to ensure a smooth experience.
