Inviting Parents to the Netherlands — Documents & Checklist

This guide lists the documents commonly required when inviting parents to the Netherlands for a short family visit (Schengen / Long-stay depending on duration). Requirements vary by consulate; use this as a comprehensive checklist and confirm with the VFS/Embassy handling your area.

1. Invitation Letter (from Sponsor in NL)

The invitation letter is the primary document. It should include:

  • Full name, date of birth, passport number and address of the invitee (parent).
  • Full name, address, contact, BSN (if available), and passport/ID of the sponsor in the Netherlands.
  • Relationship to the invitee and purpose of visit (e.g., family visit, wedding, medical).
  • Exact travel dates, duration of stay and proposed accommodation details (address where the parent will stay).
  • A clear undertaking of accommodation and financial support (if sponsor will cover costs).
  • Signature of the sponsor and date. Include scanned copy of sponsor's passport/ID and proof of residence.

2. Proof of Sponsor's Legal Status in NL

  • Copy of sponsor's passport or EU ID card (photo page).
  • Copy of Dutch residence permit or proof of permanent residence (if non-EU).
  • Copy of BSN or registration confirmation (if available).

3. Proof of Relationship

  • Birth certificate of the child showing parent's names (or the parent's birth certificate showing child's name) OR
  • Family register extract, national ID showing family relation, or marriage certificate (if inviting in-laws).

4. Invitee (Parent) Documents

  • Valid passport (min 6 months validity beyond planned stay) and a clear photocopy of main page.
  • Two recent passport-size photographs as per Schengen specs (if applying Schengen visa).
  • Completed visa application form (online or paper per VFS instructions).
  • Travel medical insurance covering travel period and Schengen minimum (€30,000) (for Schengen short-stay).
  • Round-trip flight reservation / itinerary (do not buy non-refundable tickets before visa approval).
  • Proof of accommodation — invitation letter with sponsor address OR hotel booking if staying elsewhere.

5. Financial Documents

Either the invitee shows ability to fund the trip or the sponsor provides a formal guarantee.

  • If sponsor finances the trip: a signed & dated declaration of financial support and recent bank statements (3–6 months), employment letter and payslips, or proof of savings.
  • If parent finances: recent bank statements, pension statements, or proof of regular income.

6. Additional Supporting Documents

  • Sponsor's employment letter (if employed) showing role, salary and contract period.
  • Copy of sponsor's recent payslips (3 months) or tax returns.
  • Proof of accommodation: rental agreement, home ownership papers or a letter from landlord.
  • Proof of family event (if applicable): wedding invite, medical appointment letter, funeral notice, etc.

7. Document Legalization & Translation

  • Indian documents (birth/marriage certificates) may require notarisation and MEA apostille/legalisation when requested by the Dutch authorities — check the consulate guidance.
  • Any documents not in English / Dutch may require certified translations.

8. Visa Appointment & Processing

  • Book appointment at VFS Global for the Netherlands or the embassy handling Schengen visas in your region.
  • Submit application, biometric data, and original supporting documents as required. Processing times vary (2–15 working days typical for short-stay; longer for complex cases).
  • Keep copies of everything submitted; collect passport when notified.

9. Practical Tips & Timeline

  • Start document collection 2–3 months in advance (birth certificates, legalized copies, passport validity).
  • Allow time for translations and apostille (3–6 weeks depending on your local processing).
  • Do not purchase non-refundable travel until visa is granted.

10. Sample Sponsor Declaration (brief)

"I, [Full name], resident at [address], holding passport/residence permit [no.], hereby invite my [mother/father], [full name], passport no. [xxxx], to visit me in the Netherlands from [date] to [date]. I will provide accommodation at [address] and financially support their stay if required."

Note: Embassy/consulate may request additional documents. Always follow the latest VFS/consulate checklist for your city. This guide is informational and not legal advice.
