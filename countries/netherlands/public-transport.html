Public Transport in the Netherlands

A comprehensive guide to trains, trams, buses, metro and regional transport — how to use them, pay for them, and maximize savings.

Overview: Why Public Transport is Great

  • Reliable, clean, and frequent — trains and buses run on time almost always.
  • Extensive network across the country — connects cities, towns, and rural areas.
  • Cost-effective — especially with student/youth discounts and monthly passes.
  • Eco-friendly — reduces emissions and congestion compared to car travel.
  • No parking hassles — major advantage in crowded city centres.

OV-chipkaart: The Essential Card

The OV-chipkaart is the universal payment card for all public transport in the Netherlands.

  • What it is: A rechargeable smartcard that works on trains (NS), trams, buses, metro across the country.
  • Where to buy: Train stations, supermarkets (Albert Heijn, Jumbo), tobacconists, or online.
  • Cost: €7.50 for the card itself (one-time); reload with credit as needed.
  • Recharging: At station machines, supermarkets, online via the official app, or by subscription.
  • Travel credit: Load any amount and pay-as-you-go (automatically deducted per journey).
  • Discounts: Peak/off-peak travel (off-peak 10–20% cheaper depending on distance); youth and senior discounts available.

Train Travel (NS & Regional Operators)

National Railways (NS)

  • What: Long-distance intercity (IC), regional (R), and express (Sprinter) trains connecting cities and towns.
  • When to use: Commuting between cities, weekend trips, visiting family.
  • Frequency: Multiple trains per hour on major routes; fewer in rural areas.
  • Typical costs: €2–€8 per journey (off-peak); €6–€15 peak hours, depending on distance.
  • Punctuality: Excellent (95%+); if >5 min late, you can claim compensation via NS website.

NS Subscription Options

  • Unlimited monthly pass: €93–€151/month (depending on zones) for unlimited travel during off-peak and 40% discount peak.
  • Dal Voordeel (Evening Discount): €1/month; 40% off evening/weekend travel after 6:30 PM on weekdays, all day weekends.
  • Student NS card: 40% off all journeys if enrolled in an accredited institution.
  • Young 'n' Wild (< age 30): €4/month for 40% off all journeys.

Regional Rail Operators

  • Arriva, Connexxion, Keolis: Operate regional trains in areas outside the main NS network.
  • Cost: Similar to NS, paid via OV-chipkaart or regional passes.
  • Coverage: Useful for small towns and less-connected areas.

Trams & Metro

  • Major cities: Amsterdam (GVB trams + metro), Rotterdam (RET trams + metro), Utrecht (TU trams), The Hague (HTM trams).
  • Cost: €2–€3.50 per zone; load onto OV-chipkaart.
  • Frequency: Every 5–15 minutes depending on time/line.
  • Passes: Day passes (€10–€15) or monthly passes available at ticket machines.
  • Tip: Bikes are faster for short distances in Amsterdam; trams best for longer trips or rainy days.

Buses & Coach Services

  • City buses: Local transport within towns and cities; included on OV-chipkaart.
  • Regional buses: Connect towns and villages (Arriva, Connexxion, Keolis operate most).
  • Long-distance coaches: FlixBus and others connect Netherlands to European cities; cheap alternative to trains.
  • Cost: Similar to trains; off-peak discounts available.
  • Night buses: GVB and other operators run night services in major cities (especially weekends).

Planning & Tickets

Journey Planning Apps & Websites

  • 9292.nl: Best journey planner for trains, buses, trams, metro — shows all options, costs, and transfers.
  • NS App: Focused on NS trains; buy tickets, check delays, get platform info.
  • Google Maps: Good for local tram/bus routes; less detail than 9292.
  • OV-chipkaart App: Top up credit, check balance, save favourite routes.

Buying Tickets

  • OV-chipkaart (best option): Tap on/off readers at entry/exit; automatic fare calculation; discounts applied automatically.
  • Single-journey paper tickets: Available at ticket machines or station counters (more expensive; rarely used).
  • Online (NS): Buy NS train tickets on nsinfo.nl or app; print or show digital ticket.

Connecting Transport & Transfer Tips

  • Interchange stations: Amsterdam Centraal, Utrecht Centraal, Rotterdam Centraal are major hubs.
  • Real-time updates: Delays and platform changes shown on station screens and apps.
  • Transfers: Keep OV-chipkaart ready when changing lines (tap in/out); transfer discount applies (typically €0.83 if <30 min transfer).
  • Accessibility: Most modern stations have elevators; older stations may have stairs — check accessibility info on 9292.

Commuting: Monthly Passes & Cost Savings

  • Employer discount: Many employers negotiate NS/OV deals; ask your HR.
  • Tax-free commute subsidy: Some employers offer tax-free travel allowance (up to €1.50/day in NL).
  • Bundling: NS monthly pass + OV-chipkaart credit is more economical than pay-as-you-go if commuting regularly.
  • Off-peak travel: Shift meetings/trips to evening/weekends if possible — 40% savings with Dal Voordeel.
  • Combined modality: Bike to station + train is often faster/cheaper than driving.

Bicycle + Public Transport

  • Bike + train: Most trains allow bikes (free, but space limited); busy times may be busy.
  • Bike carriage: Designated area in last/first carriage — board early.
  • Station bike parking: Cheap, secure parking at most stations (€0.50–€1.50/day).
  • OV-fiets rental: Rent a bike from stations (€4/day) — great for combining train + cycling on arrival.

Practical Tips & Etiquette

  • First class vs second class: Most commuters use second class (cheaper); first class is quieter and less crowded.
  • Rush hours: 7–9 AM and 4–7 PM on weekdays — trains/trams are packed; earlier/later travel recommended.
  • Quiet carriage: Designated quiet areas in trains — keep volume low, no phone calls.
  • Eating & drinking: Allowed, but keep mess minimal; don't eat strong-smelling food in rush hour.
  • Luggage: Large bags can be kept in luggage racks; don't block aisles or seats.
  • Delays: If >5 mins late, you may qualify for NS compensation (apply via website within 2 months).
  • Strikes: Rare, but occasional NS strikes occur; check ns.nl for notices.

Special Cases

Moving Day

  • Rent a moving van (cheaper than hiring movers); take the train to locations if far.
  • Book taxis or ride-share for large items; public transport awkward with furniture.

Airport Travel (AMS, RTM, MAA)

  • Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS): Direct train (25 min) to Amsterdam Centraal; €4–€6.50 depending on time.
  • Rotterdam (RTM): Train to city centre (15 min); €3–€5.
  • Maastricht (MAA): Train to city centre (10 min); €2–€4.
  • Uber/taxi: €30–€50+ depending on distance; recommended only for groups/luggage.
