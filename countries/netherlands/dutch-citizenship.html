Dutch Citizenship Guide

Expats living in the Netherlands for five years or more may be eligible to apply for Dutch citizenship (Nederlandse nationaliteit). Here’s a comprehensive guide.

How to Get Dutch Citizenship

  • Automatically by descent (van rechtswege)
  • Via the option procedure (optieprocedure)
  • By naturalisation (naturalisatie)

Dutch Citizenship by Descent (Van Rechtswege)

Children can automatically become Dutch citizens through birth, acknowledgement, or adoption. No application is required. Eligibility includes:

  • Born to a Dutch mother.
  • Born to a Dutch father married to or registered with non-Dutch mother.
  • Acknowledgement by a Dutch citizen before age 7.
  • Adopted by a Dutch parent before age 18.

Option Procedure (Optieprocedure)

A quicker route for certain residents who meet specific conditions, like living in the Netherlands from birth or childhood. More details on IND website.

Naturalisation (Naturalisatie)

  • Most common route for expats.
  • Approximate duration: 1 year.
  • Requirements: 18+, valid residence permit, no public security risk, willing to renounce current nationality (exceptions apply).
  • Residency requirement: usually 5 years (exceptions for marriage, statelessness, etc.).
  • Integration requirement: pass civic integration exam or be exempt.

Dual Citizenship

  • Generally, renounce previous nationality upon gaining Dutch citizenship.
  • Exceptions: married to Dutch citizen, original country rules, asylum, birth in Netherlands, etc.

Application Process

  • Apply via local municipality (gemeente) or online with DigiD.
  • Provide valid ID, residence permit, birth certificate, integration certificate, and any required translations.
  • Application fees (2026): €1,139 single, €1,454 partners, €168 per child.
  • Processing time: Option procedure ~3 months, Naturalisation ~1 year.
  • Outcome: Approval, rejection, or additional review. Attend citizenship ceremony to receive certificate.

Benefits of Dutch Citizenship

  • No residence permit required; freedom to travel and work abroad.
  • Eligibility for Dutch passport and EU mobility.
  • Right to vote and stand for election in Netherlands and EU.
  • Access to public employment (e.g., government, police, military).

Permanent Residency Alternative

If you prefer not to renounce your nationality, permanent residence allows indefinite stay under certain conditions. Learn more.
