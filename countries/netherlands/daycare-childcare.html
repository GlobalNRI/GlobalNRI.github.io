Daycare and Childcare in the Netherlands — Cost, Tax Benefits & How to Apply

This page explains how daycare and childcare work in the Netherlands, including types of childcare, monthly costs, government daycare allowance (kinderopvangtoeslag), tax implications, and when parents—especially expats—should apply.

1. Overview — Is daycare common in the Netherlands?

Yes. Daycare is widely used in the Netherlands, especially in households where both parents work. Childcare is regulated, high quality, and partially subsidized by the government through a childcare allowance.

2. Types of childcare in the Netherlands

  • Daycare (Kinderdagverblijf): For children aged 0–4 years.
  • After-school care (BSO): For school-going children aged 4–12 years.
  • Childminder (Gastouder): Home-based childcare via registered providers.
  • Preschool (Peuterspeelzaal): Short sessions for toddlers (2–4 years), often municipally supported.

3. Average daycare costs (before allowance)

Childcare in the Netherlands is expensive before subsidies.

  • Daycare (0–4 years): €8 – €11 per hour per child.
  • BSO (after school): €7 – €10 per hour.
  • Childminder: €6 – €8 per hour.

Full-time daycare can easily cost €1,500–€2,500 per month per child before allowance.

4. What is Kinderopvangtoeslag (daycare allowance)?

Kinderopvangtoeslag is a government benefit that reimburses a large part of your childcare costs. It is paid monthly by the Dutch tax authority (Belastingdienst).

5. Who is eligible for daycare allowance?

  • Both parents must be working, studying, or in an approved integration program.
  • You must have a Dutch BSN.
  • The childcare provider must be registered in the National Childcare Register (LRK).
  • You must have a DigiD.

6. How much allowance do you get?

The allowance depends on:

  • Household income.
  • Number of children in daycare.
  • Type of childcare.
  • Hourly rate (up to a government maximum).

Lower-income families can get up to 90% reimbursement. Higher-income families still receive a partial benefit.

7. When should you apply?

You must apply for daycare allowance within 3 months of your child starting daycare. Late applications may result in loss of benefits.

  • Apply as soon as you sign the daycare contract.
  • You can apply even before your child starts attending.

8. How to apply for daycare allowance

  1. Choose a registered daycare provider (LRK number required).
  2. Sign a daycare contract stating hours and hourly rate.
  3. Log in to Mijn Toeslagen using DigiD.
  4. Submit childcare details and income estimates.
  5. Receive monthly advance payments.

9. Tax return and adjustments

The allowance is based on estimated income. After the tax year ends:

  • Your actual income is calculated.
  • You may need to repay excess allowance or receive extra payment.
  • Always update income changes during the year to avoid large repayments.

10. Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using an unregistered daycare provider.
  • Not updating income or working hours.
  • Missing the 3-month application deadline.
  • Assuming allowance is automatic—it is not.

11. Practical tips for expat parents

  • Register for daycare early—waiting lists are common.
  • Keep all invoices and contracts.
  • Consider part-time daycare to reduce costs.
  • Check municipal subsidies for preschool programs.
