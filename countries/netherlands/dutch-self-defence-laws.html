Professional, highly mobile criminal groups are known to target specific communities, focusing on homes perceived to hold **significant cash and gold**. Watch out for **"casing" attempts** (e.g., strangers knocking or asking irrelevant questions) as a precursor to a break-in.

Why Criminals Target Specific Homes

The selection process is strategic, relying on high-probability indicators:

  • Perceived Asset Concentration: The belief that valuable, untraceable assets (gold jewellery and cash) are stored on-site due to cultural habits.
  • Visibility & Identification: Overt external cultural identifiers (symbols, decorations, or specific signage) simplify the process of locating the target community's homes.
  • Digital Surveillance: Monitoring public or poorly secured community platforms (WhatsApp, Facebook) to identify windows of opportunity, such as travel periods or large local events when homes will be empty.
  • Low-Risk Calculation: Burglars avoid confrontation. The penalties for simple property theft (0‚Äì4 years) are significantly lower than crimes involving violence (4‚Äì12 years).

A multi-layered defense using physical, digital, and neighbour strategies is most effective. Time is costly for burglars‚Äîmake entry difficult.

1. Door and Window Security (Hardening the Structure)

  • Certified Locks: Install locks, hinges, and fittings with the **SKG-Logo (Stichting Kwaliteit Gevelbouw)**, which indicates resistance time. Aim for **2 or 3 stars**.
  • Always Lock Up: Always turn the key at least once when leaving, even for five minutes, to prevent "shimming" (using a plastic card to force the latch). **Never leave the key on the inside of the door.**
  • Windows: Close all windows, including small, tilt-and-turn (kiepstand) windows, as these are easily hooked open.
  • Sliding Doors (Schuifpui): Secure these vulnerable entry points with an **anti-lift system** or a special lock bar.
  • Upper Floors: Secure balconies, accessible windows, and skylights, especially if reachable via a flat roof, garage, or shed.

2. Deterrence and Visibility (Making the Home Look Occupied)

  • Smart Lighting: Use timers, smart plugs, or **dusk-to-dawn switches** in multiple rooms (not just one) to simulate varying activity patterns. Burglars prefer to work in the dark.
  • Remove Climb Aids: Store all ladders, crates, bins (kliko's), and tools out of sight and away from the exterior walls, as they can be used to climb to upper floors.
  • Keep Valuables Out of Sight: Place laptops, tablets, expensive electronics, keys, wallets, and jewellery away from clear view through windows or doors.
  • Privacy for Keys: Never attach an address label to your key ring. **Never hide spare keys** under mats, flowerpots, or in the meter cupboard‚Äîburglars know these spots.

3. Financial, Digital, and Community Security

  • External Storage (Gold/Cash): Use a **certified third-party safe-deposit locker** (kluis) for high-value items, as home insurance limits for jewellery are often low (e.g., ‚Ç¨5,000).
  • Neighbourhood Watch (WABP): Join or create a **WhatsApp Buurt Preventie (WABP)** group with trusted neighbours to share alerts about suspicious activity and keep an eye on each other's homes.
  • Holiday Protocols: If away, ask a trusted neighbour to collect mail, open/close curtains, park their car in your driveway, and if possible, leave some used coffee cups or a newspaper visible to simulate presence. **Never announce your absence on social media.**

Post-Incident Protocol: Critical Actions

If you suspect or discover a break-in, follow these steps to ensure safety and preserve evidence:

  1. Safety First: DO NOT ENTER: If the intruder may still be inside, leave immediately and call 112 from a safe distance. **Do not touch anything** to preserve fingerprints and DNA evidence.
  2. Report Immediately: Call the non-emergency police line (0900-8844) for a confirmed break-in, or **112** if there is an emergency or threat. Obtain an official **case number (proces-verbaal)** for insurance.
  3. Contact Insurance: Notify your **Home Contents Insurance (Inboedelverzekering)** provider immediately. Confirm your specific policy limit for cash and jewellery (many policies cap jewellery coverage around **‚Ç¨5,000‚Äì‚Ç¨10,000**).
  4. Community Coordination: Work with neighbors and community members to file a **joint complaint** (gezamenlijke aangifte) for related incidents. This helps the understaffed police connect patterns and raises the investigation priority.

Police Prioritisation & Solvability (Modus Operandi)

The Dutch police (Politie) focus limited resources on cases with the highest solvability or threat level:

  • Violence Trigger: Any crime involving harm or violence receives the absolute highest priority and deep investigation.
  • Modus Operandi (MO) Match: Cases that align with a known, sophisticated criminal operating model (like the targeting of a specific community) are prioritised to dismantle the organized group.
  • Evidence Quality: Cases with strong forensic evidence (fingerprints, CCTV footage, DNA) have a higher chance of being pursued.
