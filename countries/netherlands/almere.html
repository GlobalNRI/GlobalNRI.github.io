Almere — City Guide for NRIs & Expats

Almere is a modern city east of Amsterdam with growing expat communities. Below are practical pointers for Indian groceries, restaurants, halal supplies, cultural meetups and useful local services.

Indian Groceries & South-Asian Stores

For local listings, try the aggregated searches below (keeps results current):

  • Google Maps — Indian grocery stores in Almere
  • Google Maps — Asian supermarkets

Common items to look for: basmati rice, atta/chapati flour, spices (masala, turmeric, garam masala), dhal/lentils, pickles, ready masala pastes, ghee, and frozen parathas/naan. Larger specialist stores in Amsterdam (35–45 mins) stock harder-to-find items.

Indian & South-Asian Restaurants

Almere has a small but growing selection of Indian and South-Asian restaurants. Use the searches below for up-to-date listings and reviews:

Halal Meat & Specialty Suppliers

Halal butchers and suppliers are available in Almere and nearby towns. If you need specific cuts, check local halal shops via Google Maps or visit larger markets in Amsterdam.

Community & Cultural Meetups

  • Facebook groups: search "Indians in Almere" or "Indian community Almere" for local meetups and events.
  • Meetup.com: look for Indian cultural, language, and food groups in Almere and Flevoland.
  • Religious centres: mosques, temples and community centres in Almere and neighboring cities host cultural and festival events—check local listings.

Practical Services

  • Supermarkets: Albert Heijn, Lidl, Jumbo — good for staples and basics.
  • Local markets: seasonal markets and international stalls sometimes offer South-Asian ingredients.
  • Courier & remittance: Wise and banks support transfers to India; compare fees and exchange rates.
  • Transport: Almere is well-connected by train to Amsterdam (20–30 minutes) for additional shopping and community options.
