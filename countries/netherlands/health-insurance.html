Health Insurance in the Netherlands — Expat Guide

This page explains how health insurance works for residents of the Netherlands, what documents and steps are needed, how to compare providers, typical premium ranges, and useful references.

1. Overview — Is health insurance mandatory?

Yes. If you are living and working in the Netherlands you are required to take out basic Dutch health insurance (the basisverzekering) from a Dutch health insurer. Some short-term visitors (tourists) may be covered by travel insurance instead. Check your residency and employment status for exact requirements.

2. What the basic package covers

  • Primary care from your general practitioner (huisarts).
  • Hospital care, specialist consultations and necessary medication (subject to GP referral and policy rules).
  • Emergency care and medically necessary treatments.

3. Deductible / excess (eigen risico)

The Dutch health system includes a statutory voluntary or mandatory deductible (called eigen risico), which applies to most care covered under the basic package. The statutory amount is set by government and can change annually — check the current year amount when you sign up. You can choose to raise voluntary deductible in exchange for a lower monthly premium.

4. Supplementary insurance (aanvullende verzekering)

If you need dental care, physiotherapy, alternative medicine or additional services, you will typically add a supplementary package. These are optional, and premiums vary by provider and level of cover.

5. Who should take which cover?

  • Employees & residents: basic package is mandatory.
  • Students on short stays: check whether student insurance or international plans apply.
  • Short visitors: travel insurance with medical cover may be sufficient; verify before travel.

6. How to compare health insurers

Use the following checklist when comparing providers:

  1. Monthly premium: Compare the basic package premium — this is the main recurring cost.
  2. Covered care: Confirm what GP, hospital and medication are covered and any referral requirements.
  3. Deductible options: Check statutory deductible and voluntary deductible levels.
  4. Supplementary options: If you need dental or physiotherapy, compare the supplementary packages.
  5. Network & reimbursement: Check whether your preferred providers are in-network and reimbursement rules for out-of-network care.
  6. Customer service & English support: For expats, check whether the insurer offers English-language service and online portals in English.
  7. Switching rules: Insurers have annual change windows; confirm deadlines and notice periods.

7. Typical premiums & examples

Premiums vary with insurer and chosen options. As a general guideline, basic package monthly premiums (per adult) commonly fall in a range — check current market sites for live rates in the year you sign up. Supplementary packages and voluntary deductible choices affect total cost. Always get quotes from comparison sites for accurate numbers.

8. How to sign up

  1. Register your address in the Netherlands and obtain a BSN (Citizen Service Number).
  2. Choose an insurer and basic package. You can sign up online; most insurers allow international cards and have English pages.
  3. Provide start date (often aligned to your registration date) — keep proof of moving and employment if requested.
  4. Set up monthly payments (direct debit) and confirm your deductible choice.

10. Switching insurers

You can switch insurers during the allowed change period (check current deadlines each year). Switching typically takes effect from the first day of the next calendar month or as per insurer rules. Keep documentation and allow time for processing.

11. Practical tips for expats

  • Choose an insurer that offers English support and straightforward online claims.
  • Check if your employer offers group contracts or contributions to premiums.
  • Consider voluntary deductible if you expect low usage — it lowers monthly premium.
  • Keep digital copies of all documents; many insurers accept scanned copies for registration.
