How to Save Money on Groceries in Australia

This guide explains practical and realistic ways to reduce your grocery bill in Australia. It covers affordable supermarkets, discount strategies, loyalty programs, weekly catalogues, local markets, and smart shopping habits—especially useful for newcomers and expats.

Top savings in four steps

  • Shop at discount chains (Aldi) and Asian/Indian grocers.
  • Use loyalty cards and check the weekly catalogue before you shop.
  • Buy private-label and bulk items intelligently.
  • Plan meals and use apps like Too Good To Go to cut waste.

1. Choose the right supermarket

Who you shop with makes a huge difference.

  • Discount: Aldi is consistently cheapest nationwide.
  • Major chains: Woolworths and Coles dominate, with frequent specials.
  • Ethnic stores: Vietnamese, Indian and Chinese supermarkets often undercut majors on rice, spices and fresh produce.

2. Buy private-label brands

Store brands are much cheaper and generally good quality.

  • Woolworths: Essentials and Macro.
  • Coles: Coles Brand and Smart Buy.
  • Aldi: most items are Aldi private label.

3. Use weekly catalogues and specials

Catalogues list the week’s best deals; available online or in‑store.

  • Review Woolworths and Coles catalogues before shopping.
  • Look for multi-buy and "two for" offers.

4. Get loyalty cards and apps

Points convert to vouchers or fuel discounts.

  • Everyday Rewards: Coles’ program synchronises with Flybuys for extra points.
  • Woolworths Rewards: points redeemable for groceries or petrol discounts.
  • Aldi Insiders: occasional digital coupons via the app.

5. Shop at farmers’ markets

Local markets can be significantly cheaper for seasonal produce.

  • Go near closing time for markdowns.
  • Many operate on weekends and Wednesday evenings.

6. Use food‑waste apps

  • Too Good To Go: surplus meals and bakery goods.
  • Olio: neighbours sharing excess food.

7. Buy in bulk selectively

  • Costco and Sam’s Club (membership) offer savings on non‑perishables.
  • Avoid large packs of perishables unless you plan to freeze.

8. Cook seasonally and meal plan

  • Use Australian seasonal produce (berries in summer, citrus in winter).
  • Plan meals around specials and discounted items.

9. Compare unit prices

Australian labels show cost per 100 g, kg or litre.

  • Compare unit prices to get the best deal.
  • Avoid paying premium for small packages.

10. Example monthly savings

Thoughtful shopping can cut a grocery budget (e.g. AU$300 single, AU$800 family) by 20–30%.

11. Practical tips for expats

  • Bring reusable bags—plastic bags cost around AU$0.15 each.
  • Self-checkout is widespread.
  • Many independent grocers and markets trade on Sundays.
