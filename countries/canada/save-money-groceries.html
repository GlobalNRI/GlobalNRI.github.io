How to Save Money on Groceries in Canada

This guide explains practical and realistic ways to reduce your grocery bill in Canada. It covers affordable supermarkets, discount strategies, loyalty programs, weekly flyers, local markets, and smart shopping habits—especially useful for immigrants and travellers.

Top savings in four steps

  • Shop at warehouse or discount chains (No Frills, Walmart) and ethnic markets.
  • Use loyalty programs and read the weekly flyer before shopping.
  • Buy private-label and bulk items with care.
  • Plan meals and reduce waste with apps like Too Good To Go.

1. Choose the right supermarket

Grocery prices vary greatly depending on the chain and province.

  • Discount/warehouse: No Frills, Walmart, Food Basics (Ontario).
  • Mid-range: Loblaws, Metro, Sobeys, Safeway.
  • Ethnic stores: South Asian, Chinese and Middle Eastern grocers often have cheaper spices, rice and produce.

2. Buy private-label brands

Store brands in Canada are called "No Name", "President's Choice", "Compliments" etc.

  • Loblaws/No Frills: No Name and President's Choice.
  • Metro: Irresistibles and Selection.
  • Sobeys: Compliments.
  • Walmart: Great Value brand.

3. Use weekly flyers and promotions

Flyers list the best deals; can be viewed online or app.

  • Digital flyers on store websites or apps (e.g., PC Optimum, Metro).
  • Compare prices using apps like Flipp or Reebee.

4. Get loyalty cards and points programs

Points convert to discounts or free items.

  • PC Optimum: used at Loblaws, No Frills, Real Canadian Superstore.
  • Air Miles: accepted at Sobeys, Safeway, Shell.
  • Metro & Food Basics: METRO & PC Optimum overlap in Quebec.

5. Shop at farmers’ markets

Seasonal produce is often cheaper and fresher.

  • Visit shortly before closing for markdowns.
  • Many markets operate Saturdays during spring–fall.

6. Use food‑waste apps

  • Too Good To Go: grocery and bakery surplus boxes.
  • Flashfood: discounts on near‑expiry items at participating stores.

7. Buy in bulk selectively

  • Bulk rice, beans, frozen vegetables at Costco or Wholesale Club (membership).
  • Only buy large quantities if you have storage and will use them.

8. Cook seasonally and meal plan

  • Canadian seasonal fruits/vegetables (apples, squash, root veg) are cheapest in autumn.
  • Plan weekly menus and buy only what you need.

9. Compare unit prices

Price tags show cost per 100 g, per kg or per litre.

  • Calculate price per unit to compare packages.
  • Avoid small packages unless they are on sale.

10. Example monthly savings

Smart shoppers can cut a grocery bill (e.g. $300 single, $800 family) by 20–30%.

ScenarioTypical spendWith smart shopping

11. Practical tips for expats

  • Bring reusable bags—many provinces charge for plastic.
  • Self-checkout is common and often faster.
  • Some stores are closed Sundays, check local rules.
