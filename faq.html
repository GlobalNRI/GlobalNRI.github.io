Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about visas, jobs, finance, and living abroad.

General Questions

What is GlobalNRI?

GlobalNRI is an information platform providing guidance on visas, jobs, cost of living, finance, and life abroad for NRIs.

Is GlobalNRI an immigration consultancy?

No. GlobalNRI provides informational content only and does not offer legal or consultancy services.

Visa & Jobs

Can I apply for a job abroad without a visa?

In most cases you need a valid work permit or visa to start employment. Some countries offer job-seeker or search visas that allow time to find work. Check the destination country's official immigration site for exact rules.

Which countries have high demand for IT professionals?

The Netherlands, Germany, Canada, Australia and parts of Scandinavia regularly hire skilled IT workers. Demand depends on specific skills (cloud, data, security) and language/visa requirements.

How do I prepare my CV/resume for international applications?

Tailor your CV to local conventions (short summary, clear dates, outcomes), highlight remote/cross-border projects, and include links to portfolios or GitHub. Consider a short cover letter explaining relocation intent.

Housing & Buying

Can NRIs buy property in the Netherlands?

Yes—non-residents can buy property, but mortgage access and taxes depend on residency, income, and lender policies. Speak with a `hypotheek` adviser early.

What are the typical buying costs I should budget for?

Common costs include: purchase price, transfer tax (may be reduced for buyers under 36), notary fees, valuation, technical inspection (~€400–800), estate agent fee (1–2%), and mortgage arrangement costs.

How long does buying a house usually take?

From offer to keys typically 6–12 weeks for straightforward transactions, longer if there are financing or survey delays. See our Paperwork & Timeline page for details.

Finance & Banking

How do I open a bank account as a non-resident?

Requirements vary: ID, proof of address, BSN (if available), and sometimes an appointment at a branch. Several banks offer expat onboarding—see our Banking & Tax page.

What is NHG and why does it matter?

NHG (National Mortgage Guarantee) reduces lender risk and can lower mortgage rates. It has eligibility limits (see our House Buying page) and a one-time fee.

Living & Local

Where can I find Indian groceries and restaurants in Almere?

Our Almere city guide lists local Indian grocers, restaurants and halal suppliers, plus helpful links to Google Maps for directions.

How do I get healthcare coverage?

In the Netherlands, residents must take out basic health insurance. New arrivals should register with a GP and choose a health insurer; see our Health Insurance page.

GlobalNRI does not provide legal or tax advice. For complex questions contact a licensed tax advisor or immigration lawyer in the destination country.
